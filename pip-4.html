Key Points of Analysis

Engagement Rate

Out of 6323 possible voters, only 460 participated, indicating a voter turnout of approximately 7.28%.

Voting Power Distribution

YES votes had significant support from a large number of stakeholders with relatively small voting power, but the bulk of the YES voting power came from a smaller group of high-PVP individuals. The top 3 individuals in the YES group had enough combined voting power to pass PIP-04.

NO votes had a more concentrated voting power among fewer individuals. The top 3 stakeholders combined 11,662,573.51 votes, with the top individual holding 8,669,816.17 PVP.

Minimal abstentions, primarily from those with lower PVP, with one significant abstainer holding 86,777.85 PVP.

Dominance of High-PVP Stakeholders

In the YES group, the top 3 individuals had enough combined voting power to pass the proposal. Similarly, the NO group had a significant concentration of voting power among the top 3 individuals, indicating a centralization of influence among a small number of stakeholders with high voting power.

Impact of Low-PVP Voters

A large number of voters with less than 500K PVP participated, but their combined influence was relatively smaller compared to the high-PVP voters. This is particularly evident in the YES votes, where 383 voters contributed only 20,142,136.50 out of 81,843,902.51 PVP.

Proposal Outcome

The proposal passed with a clear majority in both the number of votes and voting power. The high-PVP stakeholders who supported the proposal were the decisive factor in its approval.

Conclusion

The voting results for PIP-04 show a distinct pattern where a small number of high-PVP stakeholders wield significant influence over the outcome. While there is broad participation from lower-PVP stakeholders, the ultimate decision-making power rests with the top PVP holders. This dynamic highlights the importance of understanding and potentially addressing the balance of voting power within the DAO to ensure fair and representative governance.

Low voter turnout is a significant concern for the Star Atlas DAO. With only 7.28% of the possible voters participating, it questions the representativeness and legitimacy of the decisions being made. Low engagement can lead to decisions that do not reflect the broader community's interests, potentially causing discontent and disengagement among stakeholders.
